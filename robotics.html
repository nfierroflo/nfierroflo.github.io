MEOT with stereo camera
This is my MSc's Thesis project in which I've developped the 3D-INSEG algorithm generating 3D instance segmentation data from stereo pair images, thus facilitating the data association problem. The use of this data was compared to the use velodyne data showcasing its benefits in dense scenarios, obtaining better results with lower time to run.

Human Following Robot
The human-following robot project was a comprehensive endeavor focused on developing an autonomous robotic system capable of tracking and following a human operator. Beginning with the integration of LIDAR sensors for precise distance measurement, the project progressed through stages of data acquisition, storage, and processing to interpret the environment effectively. Simulations were conducted to refine algorithms for human detection and tracking, laying the foundation for real-time implementation on the robot's control system. Subsequent phases involved the integration of a camera system to detect color-coded safety vests worn by the human operator, augmenting the robot's sensory capabilities. Through a meticulous fusion of LIDAR and camera sensor data, the robot was equipped with complementary detection and tracking mechanisms, enhancing its adaptability across varied environments and lighting conditions. Rigorous testing and validation ensured the system's reliability and efficacy, culminating in the creation of a sophisticated robotic platform adept at seamlessly following a human operator with precision and autonomy.
