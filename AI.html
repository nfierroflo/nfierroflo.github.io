AI projects

Astronomical images generated with GANs
The project aimed to construct a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) model tasked with generating images depicting transient objects, specifically SuperNovae. This initiative involved integrating these newly generated examples into the classification model within the training set. Subsequently, the objective was to assess whether the incorporation of this data would yield improvements in binary classification performance. Through the deployment of the GAN model and subsequent evaluation, the project sought to explore the potential enhancement of classification accuracy facilitated by the inclusion of synthetic data depicting transient phenomena.

Human Following Robot
The human-following robot project involved developing an autonomous robotic system capable of tracking and following a human operator. It began with integrating LIDAR sensors for precise distance measurement and progressed through stages of data acquisition, processing, and algorithm refinement. Subsequent phases included integrating a camera system to detect color-coded safety vests worn by the operator. Through meticulous fusion of LIDAR and camera sensor data, the robot acquired complementary detection and tracking mechanisms, ensuring adaptability across varied environments. Rigorous testing and validation resulted in a sophisticated robotic platform adept at seamlessly following a human operator with precision and autonomy.
